I’m drawn to projects that don’t come with a playbook, the ones where figuring it out is half the job.

Way back in 2006 at CNBC, launching the site wasn’t just about pushing pixels or following a blueprint. It meant creating something that could deliver breaking news without breaking a sweat, and doing it with a team that needed the tools yesterday. It was a constant balance of building what worked, tweaking what didn’t, and finding ways to make everything click together. Some of the best moments came from improvising solutions—like crafting a custom slideshow when the off-the-shelf options didn’t cut it.

Then there was the time at CARE, where the challenge was to weave a new donation visualization into the existing flow of legacy systems. The goal: make it work, make it smooth, and make it fast. There was no guide for that—just a lot of building, testing, and refining until it fit. It meant dealing with evolving requirements and making quick adjustments without blowing the budget or the timeline. There’s something satisfying about building a solution where none existed before.

I’m open to roles that don’t fit the standard web developer mold. Maybe it’s a position that needs someone who can bridge the gap between technical development and creative vision, or a project that’s still figuring out what it needs as it goes along. If the path isn’t clear and there’s room to experiment, I’m ready to jump in and find the way forward.
